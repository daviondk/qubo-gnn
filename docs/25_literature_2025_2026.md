## AXIS 1 — Portfolio optimization via ML/DL/RL (2025–2026)
**Dominant trend:** shift from predict-then-optimize → **decision-focused / end-to-end** (differentiable
optimization layers), plus **generative (diffusion)** return/scenario models, **LLMs**, and risk beyond
Sharpe (CVaR/EVaR). **Explicit cardinality remains rare in ML** — our niche.

HIGH relevance:
- **2512.19986** Iliopoulos 2025 — Covariance-aware simplex projection for **cardinality** portfolios (classical repair op; natural baseline).
- **2502.00828** Hwang, Kong, Zohren, Lee 2025 — Decision-informed NN + LLM, differentiable opt layer (S&P100/DOW30).
- **2503.13544** Kim et al. 2025 — Decision by Supervised Learning + deep ensembles (learn optimizer output). Code: github.com/DSLwDE/DSLwDE.
- **2512.11273** Linghu, Liu, Deng 2025 — Integrated prediction + multi-period (diff. opt layer, mirror-descent fixed-point; tx-cost).
- **2601.04062** Wang & Hasuike 2026 — Smart Predict-then-Optimize, real markets, ETF 2015–25, tx-cost+turnover.
- **2605.28853** Fernandes & Desell 2026 — Financially-guided deep PO; differentiable Sharpe/Omega/**CVaR**/risk-parity; 50 S&P, Sharpe 0.29 vs −0.02.

## AXIS 3 — QUBO/Ising portfolio formulations & solvers (2024–2026)
**Trend:** from "run portfolio QUBO on quantum" → "where does quantum actually help" (answer: little for
mean-variance); **away from penalty encodings** (penalty makes graph dense/complete) → penalty-free /
feasibility-preserving; **richer constraints** (integer lots, tx-cost, turnover, CVaR, ESG).
Tier 1 (HIGH):
- **2605.17628** Lozano 2026 — **penalty-free** pipeline: cardinality penalty = dense rank-one term breaks embedding → drop penalty, classical feasibility projection. French49; regret ≤0.03%. ⭐ (our French49 baseline)
- **2605.17623** Lozano 2026 — D-Wave hybrid **audit vs Gurobi**: CQM matches proven optimum on 54 instances but ~0.68% time on QPU. ⭐ rare optimality-gap reporting.
- **2407.16468** Pugacheva et al. 2024 — **QRF-GNN** (our solver core; no portfolio experiments = our gap).
Tier 2:
- **2509.17876** Stopfer & Wagner 2025 — extensive benchmark: **MIP solves ≤1000 assets to optimality in seconds**; quantum ≈ no room. ⭐ the bar.
- **2602.14827** Mancilla et al. 2026 — constrained QAOA, **XY-mixer/Dicke** (hard cardinality), select-K then Sharpe; 10 eq, Sharpe 1.81 vs SA 1.31.
- **2502.05226** Chen, Koch et al. 2025 — large-scale **dynamic** QUBO, **integer lots + tx-cost + short-sale**, 499 S&P. ⭐ constraint-rich.
- **2507.07159** Ranabhat et al. 2025 — **Variational Neural Annealing** (autoregressive NN learned annealer), 2000+ assets, Russell 1000/3000; ~Mosek, faster. ⭐ closest learned-solver-at-scale.
- **2602.23976** Gomez Cadavid et al. 2026 (IonQ) — BF-DCQO, correlation-guided clustering decomposition, 250 S&P.
Tier 3: 2504.08843 (D-Wave hybrid cardinality), 2603.16904 (QAOA rebalance scheduling, tx-cost −44.5%),
2603.23200 (CIM block-coordinate descent), 2508.13954 (VQE Dicke, penalty-free), 2508.18625 (VQE **WCVaR**),
2508.13557 (sampling-VQA + local search, 0.49% err), 2112.03188 (CVaR/ES QUBO), 2208.11380 (index-tracking
cardinality QUBO), 2007.00017 (Mugel tensor-network), 2410.05932 (penalty-coeff estimation). Context:
2411.16431 (TN limits), 2504.03832 (QOBLIB benchmark lib), 2507.20532 (VQE/QAOA feasibility gap).
Public code: TNO-Quantum problems.portfolio_optimization (github.com/TNO-Quantum).

## AXIS 4 — PI-GNN / unsupervised neural CO (2025–2026) + the greedy-vs-GNN debate
**Genuinely new:** (1) principled relaxation→binary fixes; (2) explore/exploit & sampling solvers with
built-in refinement; (3) amortization/transfer with constraints, positioned as **warm-starts** not replacements.
Improvements:
- **2309.16965** Ichikawa 2024 (NeurIPS) — **CRA** continuous-relaxation-annealing (rounding-free; beats PI-GNN & greedy). Code: github.com/Yuma-Ichikawa/CRA4CO. ⭐ addresses our normalization artifact.
- **2507.13703** Binarizing PI-GNN (also Axis 2). ⭐
- **2405.08424** Bu et al. 2024 (ICML) — UCO for **cardinality/covering/minimum** constraints (principled differentiable handle). ⭐ our constraint.
- **2506.16732** Bu & Shin 2025 (ICML TTA) — proves **low relaxed loss ≠ good binarized solution** (our exact concern). ⭐
- X²GNN Acikalin et al. 2025 (ICLR) — explore+exploit refinement; ~1.2% on Max Clique; beats Gurobi on large under budget. Code: github.com/utkuumur/X2GNN. ⭐ (no arXiv id; OpenReview)
Sampling/diffusion/EBM: **2502.08696** scalable discrete diffusion (ICLR'25), **2406.01661** DiffUCO (ICML'24),
**2601.07792** Mancilla et al. 2026 — **THRML EBM index-tracking** (cardinality portfolio as Ising sampling; 4.31% TE vs 5.66–6.30%). ⭐ closest competitor/baseline.
Amortization/transfer: **2603.11456** UniHetCO, **2603.02462** reducibility-transfer (code: github.com/semihcanturk/COPT-MT), **2405.20543** GCON.
**The debate (must cite, hardening AGAINST pure-GNN):**
- **2602.18419** Skenderi…Ricci-Tersenghi, Angelini 2026 — hard-CSP benchmark: **classical still beats GNNs** on truly hard instances. ⭐
- **2502.03669** Wu, Zhao, Arora 2025/26 (TMLR) — KaMIS (1 CPU) beats all AI MIS; "serialization" = degree-greedy. ⭐
- **2406.11897** Nath & Kuhnle 2024 — MaxCut-Bench: many learned heuristics fail to beat greedy; ECO-DQN GNN ≈ linear regression on tabu features. Code: github.com/ankurnath/MaxCut-Bench. ⭐
- **2601.13465** Min & Gomes 2026 — "GNNs are heuristics" (single trajectory + dropout ensembling), TSP.
- Anchors: **2107.01188** PI-GNN, **2206.13211** Angelini critique, **2302.03602/2303.12096** Schuetz reply, **2207.11542** annealed training.
